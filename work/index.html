I am, primarily, a writer. You can see my portfolio of recent work at Contently. Need a writer? Email me.

Writing

I write most often about technology, but also:

  • Obsessive video game modders
  • Secretive moneyed social clubs
  • The "Phantom King of Buffalo"
  • Caffeine, alcohol, and other nerdy things.

I was an editor at Lifehacker for more than three years, writing over 5,800 posts on news, apps, and productivity. I write a regular blog on mobile and wireless topics for ITWorld, and have contributed many pieces to Fast Company's site. I wrote a post for Fast Company about what successful people do with the first hour of their day that has become almost frighteningly viral: 7,700 comments, more than 25,000 impressions each on Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and Google+, and my reply column in Twitter sees it every single day. Quite the thing.

I've written two books: The Complete Android Guide (3Ones) and Google+: The Missing Manual (O'Reilly). They were, at the time, extensive and inclusive guides to each platform, but have since fallen out of date, because personal technology now moves much faster than edition printing. I may revisit the Android guide at some point, but it's a tall order.

Podcasts

I co-hosted In Beta, a weekly podcast with Gina Trapani on Dan Benjamin's 5by5 network, for more than 75 episodes. Every so often, I get to do another episode of In Pod Form with Phil Dzikiy. I have made quite a few appearances on This Week in Google, This Week in Tech, Tech News Today, All About Android, and other TWiT shows. My cats appear in many of them, too, unbilled.

Events & Businesses

I've launched a few events and projects in Buffalo, and helped out on a few more. Namely:

  • The annual TEDxBuffalo conference, a TED-licensed all-day event with 18-minute talks about ideas worth spreading (founder/program director/sometimes organizer).
  • CoworkBuffalo, a coworking space in downtown Buffalo (co-founder/Executive Vice President of Caffeine).
  • The off-the-cuff Ignite Buffalo, an evening of informal 5-minute talks. With a bar. (founder/speaker wrangler).
  • Other people's things I like to help out on: Nickel City Ruby, BarCamp Buffalo, TEDxBuffaloWomen.
